Yellowstone Summer Adventure Itinerary

7-day geyser exploration with thermal features and wildlife

Day 1: Arrival & Geyser Country

Camper embarks on Trip

Activities:

  • Arrive via West Entrance
  • Set up camp (varies by camper type)
  • Visit Old Faithful - first eruption
  • Upper Geyser Basin boardwalk

Where You'll Stay:

  • Family Campers: Bridge Bay Campground
  • Backpackers: Backcountry Sites
  • Bikepackers: Bridge Bay (bike accessible)

Trip visits Park: Yellowstone

Travel by Path: Grand Loop Road

To Points of Interest: Trailheads for Old Faithful Area

Day 2: Thermal Features Deep Dive

Activities:

  • Grand Prismatic Spring overlook
  • Fountain Paint Pot trail
  • Midway Geyser Basin
  • Pack for backcountry departure (backpackers)

Routes Used:

  • Grand Loop Road
  • Boardwalk Trails

Day 3: Grand Canyon of Yellowstone

Activities:

  • Artist Point sunrise
  • Lower Falls viewpoint
  • Hayden Valley wildlife viewing
  • Fishing at Yellowstone Lake

Points of Interest:

  • Lower & Upper Falls viewpoints
  • Canyon Rim overlooks
  • Yellowstone Lake access

Day 4: Wildlife & Photography

Activities:

  • Early morning Lamar Valley wildlife
  • Photography workshop opportunities
  • Bird watching at various locations
  • Scenic drive through Dunraven Pass

Day 5-6: Backcountry Adventure

Multi-Day Activities:

  • Wilderness camping (all camper types)
  • Remote hot spring visits
  • Backcountry fishing opportunities
  • Dark sky stargazing

Note: Activities vary significantly by camper type during these days. Backpackers venture deep into wilderness, while family campers enjoy comfortable base camps with day hikes.

Campers travel by Paths to Points of Interest:

  • Trailheads
  • Campgrounds
  • All Points of Interest

Day 7: Departure & Reflection

Activities:

  • Final sunrise at favorite spot
  • Break camp and pack up
  • Visit ranger station for trip reflection
  • Departure via preferred route

Planning Notes

Permits & Reservations

All trips require advance planning. Campground and backcountry permits should be secured well in advance, especially for peak seasons.

Flexibility Required

Weather, wildlife, and park conditions may require itinerary adjustments. Rangers provide daily updates and recommendations.

Gear Varies by Camper

Equipment needs differ significantly between family campers, backpackers, and bikepackers. Check specific gear lists for your camping style.
