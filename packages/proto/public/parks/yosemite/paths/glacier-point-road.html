Glacier Point Road

Road is instance of Path. Itinerary travels by Path. Path starts from and ends at Points of Interest.

Route Overview

Location: South rim of Yosemite Valley

Scenic mountain road to high country viewpoints

  • Total distance: 16 miles from Wawona Road
  • Surface: Paved mountain road
  • Elevation gain: 3,200 feet (4,000 to 7,200+ feet)
  • Season: Typically May through November (snow closures)

Key Access Points

Path starts from: Wawona Road Junction

  • Connection: Highway 41 (Wawona Road)
  • Services: Chinquapin intersection

Path ends at: Glacier Point

  • Elevation: 7,214 feet
  • Features: Half Dome and valley views
  • Facilities: Restrooms, gift shop (seasonal)

Major Stops Along Route

Washburn Point

  • Point of Interest: Alternative valley view
  • Features: Half Dome perspective, less crowded
  • Distance from road: Roadside parking

Taft Point Trailhead

  • Path leads to: Taft Point overlook
  • Hiking distance: 2.2 miles round trip
  • Features: Cliff-edge views, rock fissures

Sentinel Dome Trailhead

  • Path leads to: 360-degree summit views
  • Hiking distance: 2.2 miles round trip
  • Elevation: 8,122 feet

Badger Pass Ski Area

  • Winter: Downhill and cross-country skiing
  • Summer: Trailhead for backcountry access
  • Elevation: 7,200 feet

Road Conditions

  • Winter closure: Typically closed beyond Badger Pass
  • RV restrictions: Length and weight limits apply
  • Parking: Limited at Glacier Point (arrive early)
  • Weather: Chains may be required in winter

Camping Access

  • Upper Pines Site 015 - Valley base camp
  • Bridalveil Creek Campground - High country camping (seasonal)
  • High country backcountry - Advanced access

Photography Tips

  • Sunrise: Glacier Point and Washburn Point
  • Sunset: Taft Point for dramatic lighting
  • Fall colors: Best along road in mid-October
  • Winter: Snow-covered valley views
